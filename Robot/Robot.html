Wheeled centauroid platform

We are building a robot that adapts to the workspace.

We are conceptualizing, drafting, designing, and creating a wheeled centauroid built for human workspaces, emergency inspection, and hazardous-environment utility. Our goal is to investigate how an unconventional mechatronic form can overcome common design limits by adapting to the environment instead of forcing the environment to adapt to the robot.

Project Mission

We are developing CHIRON around mobility, adjustability, manipulation, and practical field utility.

Workspace Adaptation

We want our centauroid to adapt to human workspaces rather than requiring workspaces to be redesigned around a fixed robotic platform. The adjustable base is central to that goal, giving the torso and arms access to different workbench heights and operating positions.

Adjustable HeightHuman Workspace

Wheel-Leg Mobility

We are designing a base with four articulated legs, each ending in a wheel. This structure is intended to provide stability, controlled mobility on flat or reasonably ramped floors, and the ability to reconfigure the torso height for different tasks.

Wheel-Leg BaseControlled Mobility

Dual-Arm Interaction

We are developing the torso and arm concept so the robot can perform a basic amount of interaction with its surroundings. The arms are planned as the robot's bridge between mobile inspection and practical utility in a workspace.

ManipulationMechatronics

Emergency Hazard Utility

We are aiming CHIRON toward remote assessment and response support in environments that may be unsafe for people.

Remote Hazard Detection

We want CHIRON to support emergency hazard detection by carrying sensors and inspection tools into areas where human entry could be dangerous. Potential targets include gas leaks, suspicious objects, unstable work zones, and other hazards that require distance, caution, and reliable data collection.

InspectionSensingRemote Assessment

Response and Neutralization Support

We are designing the robot as a utility platform that can assist trained operators with safe response workflows. For hazardous targets such as gas leaks, explosive threats, or other dangerous objects, our focus is on remote positioning, observation, manipulation support, and controlled interaction that keeps people farther from the hazard.

Operator SupportHazard ResponseRemote Manipulation

Field-Ready Design Thinking

We are building around practical reliability: stable mobility, serviceable electronics, modular mechanisms, and a form factor that can move through human-scale spaces. The robot is meant to be useful in real environments, not only as a demonstration of motion.

ReliabilityIntegrationUtility

Development Priorities

We are organizing the project around mechanical, electrical, and controls work that can mature together.

Mobility and Structure

We are refining the articulated wheel-leg base, torso support, height adjustment, chassis packaging, and manufacturable hardware needed to carry the robot's systems reliably.

Mechanical DesignFabrication

Controls and Electronics

We are integrating sensors, power routing, embedded controls, and software so the platform can move predictably, collect useful information, and support operator-directed tasks.

Embedded ControlsSensors

Testing and Iteration

We are using CAD, simulation, prototyping, and physical testing to reduce risk as the concept moves from design intent to working hardware.

PrototypingSystem Integration
